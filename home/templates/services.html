Services

Worship service

Every Sundays from 10 am to 12:45 pm worship service is conducted at the John Wood Scout Hall, High Wycombe (Keep Hill Rd, High Wycombe HP11 1DW) and from 3.30pm to 6pm at the Cheney School, Oxford (Cheney lane, Oxford, OX3 7QH). Due to the predominant members being from Malayalee background, services are mostly in Malayalam. However, we do provide translation and special assistance for those in need of interpretation. Pastor Finny John runs the services. For more information, please contact Pastor Finny John on 07956424509, Sibi Mathew (Oxford) on 07424 320122 or Br Mathew Issac (Aylesbury) on 07888012908.

Sunday School

“Train up a child in the way he should go, And when he is old he will not depart from it.” [Proverbs 22:6.]

We strongly believe that the church has a huge role in developing the next generation. To provide the knowledge of Christ and biblical truths, we organise weekly Sunday school services. These Sunday schools are run on the first three Sunday mornings from 9.15 am to 10am at Scouts Hall, Keep Hill Road, High Wycombe and from 3pm to 3.30pm at Oxford. The Sunday school comprises introductions, prayers, thought for the day, learning songs, completion of assignments, presentations, testimonies and also age appropriate classes. The Sunday school sessions are being run by members of the church who have been DBS checked. The Sunday school classes also provide opportunity for students to clear their doubts and seek help in spiritual matter. We also provide counselling and career support. Annual examinations are also run based on the curriculum that is being followed.

Prayer Cells

Prayer cells are the driving force for the church which propels the church forward and can be used to promote "union" and "brotherly love" between attendees. Over in three areas (High Wycombe, Aylesbury and Oxford), prayer cells are run weekly. These prayer cells run in various homes. Prayer cell forms a platform for believers to develop, share short messages, lead as well as pray for the various needs of the members. The timings and venue of the prayer cells in these areas vary depending on the convenience of the members. The Pastor visits the various prayers cells in turns.

Fasting Prayers

In High Wycombe fasting prayer meetings runs on Fridays and Saturdays of the first week of the month. And in Oxford it runs on Fridays and Saturdays of the third week of the month. These meetings are run within homes and often guest pastors are invited. Believers gather together to pray for the perishing and the needy.

Ladies Meeting

Weekly on Tuesdays ladies do gather within High Wycombe and Oxford respectively to pray. The services have helped to provide support and also share gospel. The group meets in the one of residences of the church members. We provide zoom links for those who would like to join the service online. In these meetings, ladies find time to witness Christ, encourage each other, share the word of God and pray for several needs especially for the revival and spiritual growth of individuals. Moreover, ladies as a group meet to pray for the sick, growth of the church, revival of the land and repentance of souls. They also visit families and individuals and pray for their personal and spiritual needs.

Youth meetings

Investing in today’s youth is necessary in growing the body of Christ. Teaching young people in the church to grow in their relationship with the Lord prepares them to serve Christ in all they do. As a result, this nurtures the congregation and allows the church to flourish.]

Serving young people cannot only prepare them to become future leaders, but also allow them to contribute to the church. This is seen many times in the Bible, as God often used young people to do great things. For example, God used Timothy to pastor and lead the Ephesian church when he was a teenager. 1 Timothy 4:12 says, “Don’t let anyone look down on you because you are young, but set an example for the believers in speech, in conduct, in love, in faith and in purity.”d.

Regular youth meetings are held every month on the last Sunday from 12 pm. The church members are divided into groups and take turns to display their God given talents based on their choices. The youth wing also organises annual talent tests and outdoor events.

Kingdom Kids

Yearly children activities are organised with an emphasis to create more awareness for the children and youth on spiritual matters. During these sessions special guests are invited to teach and support. The children from other churches are also invited and a theme is focused for the whole day session. These sessions also have planned games and activities.

Family conference

Yearly once family conferences are organised with a focus to strengthen fellowship among the believers and to share love. A number of spiritual, cultural events gets organised and games are run for children. The conference also provides opportunity for the families to listen to biblical teaching focussed on the ‘Christian family’ from specially invited guests.

Community services

The church collects clothes and sends them over to the needy both within and outside UK. The church supports housing projects, medical needs, education and also runs various fund raising events. The church has supported several charity organisations within UK and intends to do more
